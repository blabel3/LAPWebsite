Heroes

Here is some wisdom from experts on mental health, from professional doctors to other people who have cam eup with great strategies that work for them.

Interviews with Barbara Kellett Merk Lewis

February 9, 2017 by Blake and John

To get professional advice and first-hand information, we interviewed Barbara Kellett, who has worked with clients suffering with depression in group therapy, and Mark Lewis, who has done extensive research on nuerodevelopmental disorders and had remendous experience with depression as well.

Misconceptions

Many people have the wrong idea of depression. People can say that they need to just get over it, or that they should suck it up and just be happy. As Mark Lewis said, that's just not true. He has worked alongside many people who have counseled people with depression, and his own father had depression as well.

"There has been tremendous progress since [before 1985]. There are now much better treatments available."

Another misconception is that we haven't made any progress. AS depression is still so prevalent and it feels like more and more people get diagnosed with it or a related disorder, it can feel like things are stagnating. However, there are always doctors working to make better, more effective treatments. The scene isn't forgotten.

Public Education

Both interviewees stressed the importance of educating the general public. Having a support group of friends and family that understand can make the entire process so much better, but first that support group has to understand what the struggle is like. This public awareness can come from community leaders, teachers, political leaders, the media... all the ways that people can learn. It doesn't matter, so long as we can finally get rid of the nasty stigmas around talking about depression.
